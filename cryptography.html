🔐 Cryptography

A guided, runnable tour of the cryptography that secures real systems — each method from scratch, generic and framework-free, with code you execute right in the page. Work through it in order, or jump to what you need.

Every module is self-contained and offline-capable: plain-English intuition, the math (often small enough to check by hand), and live ▶ Run demos plus hands-on calculators using the browser's native WebCrypto — no libraries, no build step.

The through-line

The first six modules aren't a random list — they're the pieces of one machine. This is how every HTTPS connection actually protects your data, and the capstone assembles them end to end:

            agree a key          derive keys         encrypt data
   ECDH ───────────────▶ HKDF ───────────▶ AES-GCM
   (key agreement)      (key derivation)   (authenticated encryption)
        │                                        ▲
        │  Hashing/HMAC = integrity everywhere   │
        └── RSA / Signatures = identity & trust ──┘

               ⇓  all assembled in  ⇓
                  TLS 1.3 handshake
Core series · start here
1
🤝ECDH — Key Agreement
How two strangers agree on a shared secret over an open line. The foundation — includes a step-by-step number calculator, an ECIES encrypt→decrypt playground, and a man-in-the-middle attack you run yourself.
→ 2
🧬Hashing & HMAC
Digital fingerprints and keyed authentication — one-way hashes, the avalanche effect, and proving who sent a message. Live hash box and an HMAC sign→verify tool.
→ 3
🔒AES-GCM
The symmetric workhorse that actually encrypts the data — one key, a tamper seal, and the never-reuse-a-nonce rule. Encrypt and decrypt your own text.
→ 4
🧪HKDF & PBKDF2
Turning a raw secret or a password into clean, purpose-specific keys — the missing middle between ECDH and AES-GCM. Derive your own keys and watch domain separation.
→ 5
🔑RSA
Public-key encryption and signatures via the factoring problem — the classic contrast to elliptic curves. Tiny by-hand RSA plus a build-your-own-key calculator.
→ 6
✍️Digital Signatures
Proving authorship and integrity — sign with a private key, verify with the public one. ECDSA & EdDSA, the nonce-reuse footgun, and a sign→verify playground.
→
Capstone
7
🔗TLS 1.3 Handshake
Where it all comes together: ephemeral ECDHE, a transcript hash, the HKDF key schedule, a certificate signature, and AES-GCM records — the machinery behind every 🔒. Run a whole handshake and watch forward secrecy live.

Bonus modules
8
🧩Shamir's Secret Sharing
Split a secret into n shares so any k rebuild it and k−1 reveal nothing — how wallet seeds and root keys are guarded. Split and recombine your own secret.
→ 9
🎭Zero-Knowledge Proofs
Prove you know a secret while revealing nothing — the Ali Baba cave, the Schnorr protocol, and the idea behind zk-SNARKs. Prove honestly, then try (and fail) to fake it.
→
